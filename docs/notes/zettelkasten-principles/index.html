Zettelkasten Principles

Core principles and practices of the Zettelkasten method for knowledge management.
May 18, 2023 · 2 min · 222 words

Main Idea#

The Zettelkasten method is a specific approach to knowledge management developed by Niklas Luhmann that emphasizes atomic notes and organic connections. Understanding its core principles can help create a more effective personal knowledge system.

Core Principles#

1. Atomicity#

  • Each note contains a single, coherent idea
  • Short enough to be focused, long enough to be self-contained
  • Enables flexible recombination of ideas

2. Connectivity#

  • Notes gain value through connections to other notes
  • Links should be meaningful and explain the relationship
  • The network is more valuable than individual notes

3. Emergence#

  • Structure evolves organically rather than being pre-planned
  • Categories and themes emerge through connections
  • The system becomes “networked thought” rather than hierarchical filing

Implementation Practices#

Writing Effectively#

  • Write notes in your own words (avoid copy-pasting)
  • Make each note understandable on its own
  • Consider future retrieval and connections when writing

Creating Connections#

  • Reference related notes explicitly with links
  • Create “hub notes” that organize concepts thematically
  • Revise and update connections as your understanding evolves

Long-term Development#

  • Regularly review and refine notes
  • Follow chains of thought through connections
  • Look for unexpected patterns and insights
